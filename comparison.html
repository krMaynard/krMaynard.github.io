DSA Reports vs. SoR Database

Every EU DSA Very Large Online Platform must both file a biannual transparency report (tables 3–11) and submit individual Statements of Reasons (SoRs) for each content-moderation decision to the EU DSA Transparency Database in near real-time. This page independently aggregates SoRs by platform and category, then compares the totals against what each platform published in its H2 2025 transparency report. Discrepancies may indicate reporting gaps, category differences, or submission delays — not necessarily misconduct.

Methodology: "Reported" figures come from platform-published H2 2025 DSA transparency reports (t4: notices by category; t5: own-initiative illegal content actions). "SoR" figures are independently aggregated from EUDSATDB Statements of Reasons for the same period using source_type and category fields. The comparison covers only t4 notices and t5/t6 own-initiative actions — tables t3/t7–t11 have no SoR equivalent. Delta = |reported − sor| / max(reported, sor).
