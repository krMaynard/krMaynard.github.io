Case study

DSA Transparency Reports — VLOPs & VLOSEs

Under the EU Digital Services Act, Very Large Online Platforms (VLOPs) and Very Large Online Search Engines (VLOSEs) must publish biannual transparency reports on content moderation. This project aggregates H2 2025 reports from 25 services into a single cross-platform dashboard, covering all 22 VLOPs active during this period and the 2 VLOSEs across all 9 DSA report tables: notices, own-initiative actions, government orders, appeals, automated means, human resources, user reach, and qualitative descriptions.

Sources: AliExpress, Amazon, Apple (App Store), Bing, Booking.com, LinkedIn, Meta (Facebook, Instagram), Pinterest, Pornhub, SHEIN, Snapchat, Temu, TikTok, Wikipedia, X, XNXX, XVideos, YouTube / Google (6 services), and Zalando. Raw source files: github.com/krMaynard/dsa-transparency-data.

Reading the data

  • Notices (Table 4) are submissions from users or trusted flaggers reporting allegedly illegal content. A single notice can reference multiple items.
  • Own-initiative actions (Tables 5 & 6) are measures taken proactively by a platform without a notice — Table 5 covers illegal content, Table 6 covers policy violations.
  • Government orders (Table 3) are orders from EU member states to act against content or provide user information.
  • Appeals (Table 7) covers complaints submitted to a platform's internal mechanism and out-of-court dispute settlement bodies.
  • Automated means (Table 8) reports volume of moderation actions taken by automated means versus human review, along with accuracy, precision, and recall metrics per service and language.
  • Human resources (Table 9) reports the number of internal and external content moderators employed by each platform, including a breakdown by language.
  • User reach (Table 10) reports the average monthly active recipients (AMAR) per EU member state. Some platforms report approximate figures: Booking.com uses ranges (midpoint shown); Temu uses "M" notation; Google services link to a separate MAR report and are omitted.
  • Qualitative information (Table 11) contains each service's free-text descriptions of its content moderation governance, automated means, and human resources.
  • TOTAL rows aggregate all content categories.
  • Empty cells in the source data are treated as zero or not applicable. Contextual notes from individual platforms are not included in this view.

Data: official DSA transparency reports, H2 2025. Analysis by Kieran Maynard. No analytics or tracking beyond the site default.
