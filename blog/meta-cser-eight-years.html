Meta Built an Enforcement Machine for Eight Years. In 2025 It Turned the Dial Down.

July 14, 2026
Transparency Data

Meta reports it actioned 13.2 million pieces of hateful-conduct content across Facebook and Instagram in Q4 2024 — and 2.6 million in Q4 2025, an 80% drop that lands almost exactly on the company's January 2025 enforcement overhaul.

Bottom line: Thirty-three quarters of Meta's voluntary Community Standards Enforcement Report show two eras: an eight-year build-up in which proactive, automated enforcement rose from a minority of removals to 85–97% across most policy areas, then a sharp 2025 reversal concentrated in exactly the areas Meta said it would loosen — hateful conduct (-59% actioned year over year on quarterly averages), violence and incitement (-77%), and bullying and harassment (-59%) — while Meta's own prevalence estimate for bullying and harassment on Facebook rose from 0.06–0.07% of views in Q4 2024 to 0.09% by Q4 2025. The big caveat is baked in: every number here is Meta measuring Meta, and part of the drop reflects a narrower policy (less content counts as violating), not just less enforcement.

  • Data: cser_metrics, Transparency Report API, quarterly 2017 Q4 – 2025 Q4 (Facebook + Instagram).

Eight years of building the machine

The report opens in late 2017 with Facebook actioning 1.6 million pieces of hate speech in a quarter, only 23.6% of it flagged by Meta's systems before users reported it. The build-up from there is steep: by Q4 2020 Facebook's proactive rate on hateful conduct reports at 97.1%, and content actioned across all policy areas except Fake Accounts and Spam grows from 24.7 million (Q4 2017) to a plateau around 160–200 million per quarter from 2021 through 2024. (Raw totals are dominated by composition: Fake Accounts — 1.08 billion accounts actioned per quarter on average in 2024 — plus Spam make up roughly 90% of the headline number, which is why the ex-Fake/Spam series is the one worth watching.)

January 2025: the dial turns

In January 2025 Meta announced it would narrow its Hateful Conduct policy, rely less on proactive automated sweeps for "lower-severity" violations, and prioritize reducing enforcement mistakes. The data shows the turn, quarter by quarter, on both apps:

Policy area (FB+IG, Content Actioned, avg/quarter) 2024 2025 Change
Violence and Incitement 16.6M 3.9M -77%
Bullying & Harassment 16.6M 6.8M -59%
Hateful Conduct 14.6M 5.9M -59%
Adult Nudity & Sexual Activity 53.2M 39.9M -25%
Spam 487.7M 276.7M -43%
Suicide, Self-Injury & Eating Disorders 13.1M 17.0M +30%
Child Endangerment: Nudity & Physical Abuse 2.0M 3.3M +64%
All areas ex Fake Accounts & Spam 171.0M 116.1M -32%

The decline is progressive, not a one-quarter step: hateful-conduct actions fall 13.2M → 9.5M → 8.4M → 3.2M → 2.6M from Q4 2024 to Q4 2025. By year-end, both apps combined were actioning less hateful-conduct content than Facebook alone did in Q4 2017. Meanwhile areas Meta flagged as still-high-severity moved the other way — child endangerment and suicide/self-injury actions rose.

The proactive rate tells the same story from the other side:

Facebook proactive rate (%) 2018 Q4 2021 Q4 2024 Q4 2025 Q3 2025 Q4
Hateful Conduct 60.7 95.9 95.0 66.1 74.0
Bullying & Harassment 20.7 58.8 85.2 63.8 70.5
Violence and Incitement — 96.6 97.5 70.6 80.8
Dangerous Organizations: Terrorism 99.6 97.7 99.4 98.7 98.8

Terrorism enforcement — untouched by the policy change — stays above 98%. The loosened areas give back a decade of proactive-rate gains in three quarters, with a partial rebound in Q4.

Did anything get worse?

Meta's prevalence metric (the estimated share of content views that violate policy) is the closest thing to an outcome measure. Hateful-conduct prevalence on Facebook stayed flat through 2025 at 0.01–0.02% of views. Bullying and harassment did not: 0.06–0.07% in Q4 2024, 0.09–0.10% by Q3 2025, 0.09% in Q4 — back to levels last reported in 2022. Violence-and-incitement prevalence held at 0.02–0.03%. So on Meta's own measurements, the pullback was mostly free — except where it wasn't.

It's worth noting what this voluntary report cannot show: it is global aggregates at app × policy × quarter, with no country breakdown, no per-decision records, and definitions Meta can change mid-series — a grain far coarser than what the EU's DSA now compels from the same company.

Caveats. All figures are Meta's self-reported and self-measured numbers ("Meta reports…"), latest period 2025 Q4. "Hateful Conduct" was itself redefined and narrowed in January 2025, so falling actions partly reflect a smaller violating set, not only lighter enforcement; the flat prevalence figure is measured against the new, narrower policy. Content Actioned counts mix media types and include Fake Accounts (accounts, not content) — excluded above along with Spam where noted. Prevalence figures are ranges (lower/upper bounds), never additive, and are unavailable for some areas and early years. Cross-Policy aggregate rows were excluded from all sums.

Data: cser_metrics, Transparency Report API, 2017 Q4 – 2025 Q4.
