A Record 1.35 Million Complaints, a 0.07% Action Rate: X's Turkish Takedown Ledger

July 14, 2026
Transparency Data

Under Türkiye's Law No. 5651, X reports receiving 1.35 million individual removal requests in the first half of 2025, an all-time high, while actioning just 0.05% of "Abuse" and 0.07% of "Hateful Conduct" complaints, the two categories that made up 88% of the flood.

Bottom line: X's six-monthly Law 5651 reports show Turkish individual takedown requests (Art. 9/9-A personality- and privacy-violation filings) nearly quadrupling from 348k in H1 2021 to a record 1.35M in H1 2025, and they show a striking split in what gets actioned: copyright complaints have been actioned at 69–92% every period since 2022, while the mass-volume speech categories collapsed to near zero in H1 2025, though all of this is X's own self-reported accounting, and the near-zero rates could reflect a reporting or definitional change as much as a policy one. Meta's reports for the same regime are far coarser, no category breakdown at all, and show an individual stream roughly 3,000 times smaller.

  • Data: turkey_metrics, Transparency Report API, X H1 2021–H1 2025; Meta (Facebook/Instagram) H1 2023–H1 2025; TikTok through H2 2025.

The flood, and what it's made of

X is the only platform in this dataset that breaks its individual-request stream down by issue category, and the composition has shifted hard toward speech complaints. In H1 2021, "Private Information" and "User Impersonation" led the ledger; by H1 2025, "Hateful Conduct" (623,877 requests) and "Abuse" (563,954) together accounted for 88% of all individual requests, up from 23% in H1 2021.

The volatility is real: H1 2023 saw only 51,768 requests, then H2 2023 jumped 17-fold to 888,989, driven by "Right to Privacy" (312,823) and "Private Information" (438,908), a spike pattern consistent with mass-filing campaigns rather than steady organic complaint flow.

What X actions, and what it doesn't

The per-category rates in H1 2025 are the sharpest split in the whole series:

Category (2025 H1) Requests Action rate
Hateful Conduct 623,877 0.07%
Abuse 563,954 0.05%
Private Information 126,984 63.5%
User Impersonation 21,175 15.2%
Copyright 8,825 76.9%
Right to Privacy 8,060 4.6%

Copyright is the constant: never actioned below 35.6% (H1 2021), and between 69% and 92% in every period since 2022, the one category where a Turkish complainant reliably gets a result. The speech categories are the reversal: X reported actioning 71.3% of Abuse complaints in H1 2024 and 52.9% in H2 2024, then 0.05% in H1 2025 even as Abuse volume more than doubled to 563,954. Because Private Information (63.5%) and Copyright (76.9%) look normal in the same report, this is not a units glitch. Whether it reflects a genuine enforcement stop, a flood of complaints X deemed non-violating, or a change in how it counts, only X knows.

Meta reports a different report

Meta discloses at report-level totals only, no categories. Its individual stream is tiny by comparison: Facebook reports 298 applications received in H1 2025 and Instagram 162, against X's 1.35 million requests. Meta's authority stream (Art. 8/8-A) is where its numbers move: ICTA requests jumped from 2,350 in H2 2024 to 9,126 in H1 2025, a figure that appears identically in both the Facebook and Instagram reports, so it is a bundled Meta-wide count, not additive. Facebook reports entities removed under authority requests rising from 1,218 (H2 2024) to 5,580 (H1 2025), with restrictions at 3,745.

Caveats. Everything here is self-reported by the platforms under Additional Article 4 of Law 5651; latest period is H1 2025 (TikTok, which publishes only bare application counts, 259 in H2 2025, extends one period further). Aggregates are composition-dominated: H1 2025's total and near-zero derived rate are driven almost entirely by two categories. X's H2 2021 report lists "Deceased Users" at 43,429 requests with the same 24.0% rate as "Hateful Conduct" at 43,430, an apparent duplication that inflates that half's total. Action rates are percentages and were never summed; overall rates above are volume-weighted derivations. Meta's ICTA and some authority counts bundle Facebook and Instagram, and its request components do not always sum to requests_total.

Data: turkey_metrics, Transparency Report API, H1 2021–H2 2025.
