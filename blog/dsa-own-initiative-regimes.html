One Platform, Three Definitions of "Proactive"

July 14, 2026
EU DSA VLOP Transparency Data

Meta tells the EU it detects 95% of enforced content automatically. It tells India its *proactive rate* is 99% for spam — and 69% for violence. Same company, same year, incomparable numbers, because each regime defines "proactive" differently and discloses it at a different grain.

Bottom line: "Proactive," "automated," and "own-initiative" are not portable metrics. Under India's IT Rules, Meta must publish a proactive-detection rate per policy area — and it reveals a huge spread: Facebook proactively catches 99% of spam, 99% of child-sexual-exploitation, 99.7% of suicide/self-injury content, but only ~69–71% of hate speech, violence, and bullying. Under the DSA, Meta publishes a single automated-detection flag (~95% for Facebook) with no category breakdown — and the database's marginal-cut design structurally cannot produce one. The regime that looks less prescriptive (India, one country) exposes exactly the weak spots — the contested speech harms — that the DSA's aggregate hides.

  • Data: india_metrics (IT Rules 2021 monthly filings, Meta, latest 2023-09) vs dsa_tdb_metrics (H2 2025). Transparency Report API. Part 4 of the own-initiative series (Part 1 · Part 2 · Part 3).

India makes Meta show its work, by policy area

India's IT Rules require significant social-media intermediaries to report, each month, the volume of content "actioned proactively" and a proactive rate for each policy area. Facebook's filing:

Policy area (Facebook, India IT Rules) Proactive rate
Suicide and Self-Injury 99.7%
Child Endangerment – Sexual Exploitation 99.2%
Spam 99.2%
Violent and Graphic Content 98.5%
Regulated Goods: Drugs 98.5%
Dangerous Organizations: Terrorism 98.3%
Adult Nudity and Sexual Activity 96.4%
Child Endangerment – Nudity/Physical Abuse 94.3%
Bullying and Harassment 70.8%
Hate Speech 71.0%
Violence and Incitement 69.1%

The pattern is the real finding: proactive detection is near-total for content with a clear signal (spam, known CSAM hashes, nudity classifiers) and drops ~30 points for the context-dependent harms — hate, harassment, incitement — where a machine can't safely decide alone. Instagram's rates run higher (bullying 98.9%, hate 94.8%), which is itself informative: the same company proactively catches harassment far better on one surface than another. None of this is a single "proactiveness" number.

The DSA can't show you this

Read Meta's DSA disclosures for the same behaviour and you get:

  • **One automated-detection rate** — Facebook ~95%, from the SoR automated_detection flag (Part 3).
  • No category split of it. The transparency database publishes by_category and by_automated_detection as separate marginal cuts — there is no category × automation joint. You cannot ask "what share of Facebook's hate speech actions were automated?" of the DSA data. The question India answers by design, the DSA structurally cannot.
  • A different denominator entirely — India's rate is "% of actioned content caught before a user report"; the DSA flag is "% of decisions where automated means identified the content," over EU traffic, on a broader content base. The two ~95%/~99% headline numbers are not measuring the same thing and cannot be reconciled.

So a regulator wanting to know "how well does Meta proactively catch hate speech?" — the policy-relevant question — gets a usable answer from India's one-country regime and no answer at all from the DSA's continent-wide one. The DSA's aggregate 95% launders the 69% into invisibility.

The general lesson for the series

This is the cross-regime version of the Part 1–2 problem. "Own-initiative / proactive / automated" is defined:

  • by outcome grain — India per policy area; DSA aggregate;
  • by axis — India "before a user report"; DSA "by automated means"; Part 3's "decision" vs "detection";
  • by scope — India traffic vs EU traffic vs global voluntary reports.

Stack a platform's disclosures across regimes and you don't get a richer picture of one quantity — you get several different quantities wearing the same word. Any claim of the form "Platform X is Y% proactive" is meaningless without naming the regime, the axis, the grain, and the denominator.

Caveats. India's proactive_rate in this dataset is Meta-only and latest 2023-09 (Meta's IT Rules cadence; other Indian filers report volumes, not rates) — so this is an illustrative cross-regime contrast, not a live head-to-head. The DSA figures are H2 2025. Both are self-reported and unaudited.
