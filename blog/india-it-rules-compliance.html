Five million complaints, or 723? India's grievance reports don't add up

July 14, 2026
Transparency Data

Under one law, ShareChat reported over 5 million user complaints in a month while Twitter reported 723 — because "grievance received" means something different on every platform.

Bottom line: India's IT Rules 2021 require every large social-media intermediary to publish a monthly count of user grievances received and actioned, plus its dealings with the Grievance Appellate Committee (GAC). But the eight platforms here count on wildly incompatible scales: ShareChat and Moj report millions of "complaints" a month (apparently every in-app report), Google and Meta tens of thousands of formal grievances, and Twitter and Roblox a few hundred to a few thousand — roughly four orders of magnitude for the same line item. The numbers are self-reported and real, but not comparable, and most platforms have stopped filing.

  • Data: india_metrics (grievances, grievances_received, complaints, complaints_received, gac_appeals, gac_orders), Transparency Report API, monthly/half-yearly filings 2021-04 to 2026-05.

The grievance channel: same law, incomparable counts

Each platform reports a monthly "grievances received" figure, but the definitions diverge completely. ShareChat and Moj file a single lump number in the millions with no category breakdown — consistent with counting every in-app abuse report. Google, Facebook, and Instagram report tens of thousands, sorted into their own taxonomies (Google by legal category, Meta by user-facing issue type). Twitter and Roblox report only formal grievances routed to a Grievance Officer, in the hundreds or low thousands. No two taxonomies match, and the category schemes don't even line up in kind: Facebook's "Fake profile that's pretending to be me" is a user's complaint type; Google's "Copyright" is a legal basis; Roblox's "Violent Content and Gore" is a policy area.

The gap is not marginal. In its final filing ShareChat reported about 6,900 times more grievances than Twitter did in its own last month — both in 2022, both under the identical rule. Averaged over each platform's reporting life, ShareChat ran about 6.4 million grievances a month against Twitter's 474, a ratio near 13,000-to-1. This is a definitional artifact, not a measure of user unhappiness: a platform that logs every "report" button press will dwarf one counting only escalations to a designated officer.

Coverage is just as fragmented. Google has filed continuously for 62 months and still files (21,344 grievances in May 2026). Everyone else has gone dark: ShareChat and Moj stopped in April 2022, Twitter in October 2022, Facebook and Instagram in September 2023. Roblox began only in March 2025 — and even rewrote its category list mid-stream, producing 40 labels over 15 months. No single month has all eight platforms reporting, so a true cross-platform comparison is impossible.

The GAC: a tribunal almost nobody appeals to

The 2023 rules created a Grievance Appellate Committee where users can appeal a platform's grievance decision. In this dataset only Google reports appeal outcomes, and only Google and Meta report at all. Over three years the GAC closed 314 appeals of Google decisions — and sided with the appellant in just 22 of them.

Appeal volume is climbing — from 12 closed in the first half-year to 120 in the latest — but the allow rate sits at just 7%, and nearly as many appeals (136) are dismissed as "not admitted" as are rejected on the merits (156). Meta separately reports GAC orders: 26 received across four scattered months of 2023, all 26 complied with, then no further filings. Against grievance volumes in the tens of thousands to millions, appellate review is a rounding error.

Caveats. Every figure here is self-reported by the platform and unaudited; "grievances received" is defined differently by each filer, so the cross-platform totals must not be summed or ranked as like-for-like. Latest-month values are drawn from each platform's most recent filing, which for most platforms is now years stale — only Google and Roblox report into 2026. Google files GAC appeals in half-year buckets while Meta files monthly, and only Google reports appeal outcomes; the "allowed/rejected/not admitted" columns are additive within Google's data (they sum to closed) but cover no other platform.
