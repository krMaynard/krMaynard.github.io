What TikTok Volunteers, and What Governments Make It Say

July 14, 2026
Transparency Data

TikTok's voluntary enforcement report counts 2.94 billion videos removed since mid-2020 — while the ledger it publishes because governments ask, its legal-requests report, shows demands up more than 700-fold since 2019.

Bottom line: TikTok reports two very different transparency streams. In its voluntary Community Guidelines Enforcement Report, removals have quadrupled — from 43.1M videos in 2020 Q3 to 184.0M in 2026 Q1 — with a reported proactive-removal rate now at 99.3%. In the government-and-legal-requests stream, content-removal demands grew from 28 in 2019-H1 to 20,175 in 2025-H2, and legal user-data requests from 250 to 22,223. The biggest caveat sits in composition: nearly a third of all 2025-H2 removal demands came from one country, Malaysia, and every number here is TikTok's own self-reported figure.

  • Data: tiktok_cger_metrics (quarterly, 2020 Q3 – 2026 Q1, Global) and tiktok_metrics (half-yearly, 2019-H1 – 2025-H2, by country), Transparency Report API.

The volunteered ledger: 2.9 billion videos, almost none of them seen

Across 23 quarters, TikTok reports removing 2,944,514,725 videos for Community Guidelines violations. The trajectory is steadily upward, with a peak of 211.2M in 2025 Q1:

Quarter Videos removed Proactive removal rate Removed within 24h
2020 Q3 43,086,543 93.4% 93.8%
2021 Q3 91,445,802 95.1% 93.9%
2022 Q3 110,954,663 96.5% 92.7%
2023 Q3 136,530,418 96.1% 90.6%
2024 Q3 147,875,561 98.2% 92.1%
2025 Q3 204,534,932 99.3% 94.8%
2026 Q1 184,012,576 99.3% 94.4%

The proactive rate — removals initiated before any user report — climbed from 93.4% to 99.3%, meaning user flags now precede well under 1% of removals. TikTok's "removed before any views" rate was reported through 2023 Q3 (85.3% in 2020 Q3, drifting down to 76.8%); from 2023 Q4 the report switched to view-band shares, under which the zero-views share of removals rose from 77.2% to 82.2% in 2026 Q1 (peaking at 90.5% in 2025 Q2). One definitional warning: the new policy-level breakdown (populated from late 2025) doesn't sum to the headline — the seven policy categories for 2026 Q1 add to about 204M against a 184M total, so a video evidently can count against multiple policies. The largest single category is Regulated Goods, Services, and Commercial Activities at 69.8M removals.

The compelled ledger: 720-fold growth, concentrated demand

The government-and-legal stream is microscopic next to the voluntary one — and exploding. Government content-removal requests went from 28 in 2019-H1 to 20,175 in 2025-H2; legal requests for user information from 250 to 22,223; emergency data requests from 48 to 3,781.

Composition matters enormously here. Of the 20,175 removal requests in 2025-H2 (spread across 102 reported countries), Malaysia alone filed 6,302 — 31.2% of the world total, up from just 20 requests in 2022-H1. Even excluding Malaysia, requests still grew steeply (13,873 in 2025-H2). The rest of the top five: Singapore (1,737), Indonesia (1,113), Russia (947), Pakistan (935).

User-information demands skew entirely differently — toward Western legal systems:

Country (2025-H2) Legal requests Share disclosed
Germany 5,109 74.3%
United States 4,586 58.1%
United Kingdom 2,902 72.1%
France 2,681 70.5%
Spain 939 54.0%

Where the streams meet: TikTok's own rules do most of the work

The most telling number links the two ledgers. In 2025-H2, government removal requests specified 113,261 pieces of content. TikTok reports actioning 58,763 of them under its Community Guidelines and only 23,937 under local law, leaving 30,561 not actioned. So even when a government points at specific content, roughly 71% of what gets removed falls to the same voluntary rulebook driving the 184M-per-quarter machine — the local-law lever is the minority path. For scale: all governments combined flagged about 113 thousand items in six months, while TikTok's own enforcement removed roughly 360 million videos over a comparable stretch — a gap of more than three orders of magnitude.

Caveats. All figures are TikTok's self-reported numbers, unaudited. The CGER is voluntary and Global-only here (latest: 2026 Q1); the legal-requests data runs half-yearly with per-country detail only from 2022-H1 (latest: 2025-H2). Rates were never summed; the "before any views" metric was discontinued after 2023 Q3 and replaced by view-band shares, so those series aren't directly comparable. Malaysia's 31% share dominates the 2025-H2 removal-request total, and the new policy-category breakdown double-counts multi-policy videos relative to the headline removal figure.
